# üîπ **What Is a Sales Funnel?**

A **sales funnel** describes the process a potential customer travels from first hearing about your brand to becoming a paying customer ‚Äî and ideally a loyal repeat buyer. Funnels are structured with multiple stages to guide prospects through awareness, evaluation, purchase, and loyalty. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Typical Funnel Stages:**

1. **Awareness** ‚Äì Prospects discover your brand.
2. **Discovery** ‚Äì They start exploring what you offer.
3. **Evaluation** ‚Äì They compare options and consider your product.
4. **Intent** ‚Äì They decide if your offer meets their needs.
5. **Purchase** ‚Äì They buy (checkout or form submission).
6. **Loyalty** ‚Äì Repeat purchases and long-term engagement. ([optimizepress.com][1])

## üìç **1. Squeeze Page Funnel**

* Focus: Capture **email/contact info** quickly.
* Structure: Landing page ‚Üí Thank-you page.
* Exchange: Valuable content (lead magnet) for a visitor‚Äôs email.
* Purpose: Build subscriber lists for future nurturing and offers. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Example Use:** Offer a PDF guide in exchange for an email. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **2. Reverse Squeeze Page Funnel**

* Inverse of the traditional squeeze page.
* Provide **high-value content first**, then ask for an email once trust is built.
* Often boosts opt-in conversions because value precedes the ask. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**How it Works:**

1. Publish in-depth content.
2. Include free resources (templates, checklists).
3. Then prompt email opt-in for more. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **3. Lead Magnet Funnel**

* Goal: **Generate leads** by offering content or a resource.
* Funnel mimics standard awareness ‚Üí interest ‚Üí evaluation ‚Üí decision.
* Useful at multiple stages: TOFU (ads/content), MOFU (nurturing), BOFU (conversion). ([optimizepress.com][1])

**TOFU / MOFU / BOFU Breakdown:**

* Top: Broad awareness, ads, social media.
* Middle: Engaged prospects seek more info (webinars, demos).
* Bottom: Ready to buy with offers, trials, proposals. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **4. Webinar Funnel**

* Use a **live or pre-recorded webinar** to educate and build authority.
* In exchange for registrations, you gather leads.
* The webinar nurtures the prospect and leads to eventual sales. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Components:**

* Landing page with registration
* Confirmation and reminder emails
* Webinar content
* Follow-up emails with offers. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **5. Survey Funnel**

* Uses surveys to **qualify leads and gather data**.
* Questions filter out unqualified visitors.
* After completion, deliver incentives (discounts, content) and follow-ups. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Benefits:**

* Better audience insights
* Segment leads for personalised offers. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **6. Limited Time Offer Funnel**

* Leverages **urgency and scarcity** to prompt buyer action.
* Offers discounts, bonuses, or special deals for a short period. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Key Elements:**

* Pre-launch teasers
* Countdown timers
* Sales pages emphasising urgency. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Warning:** Extending offers too long reduces urgency. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **7. Daily Deal Funnel**

* Offers a **highly discounted product** for a very short time (e.g., 24‚Äì48 hours).
* Designed to get a surge of customers at once.
* After purchase, upsell to higher-priced items. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Use Case:** Similar to Groupon-style promotions ‚Äî impulse buys with upsells. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **8. Product Launch Funnel**

* A sequence of content leading up to a **new product release**.
* Builds excitement, collects waitlist sign-ups, and then opens buying for a short window. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Strategy:**

* Pre-launch content to create anticipation.
* Landing page for waitlist.
* Launch offer with urgency. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **9. Tripwire Funnel**

* Offers a **low-price, irresistible first offer** (tripwire) to get a first purchase.
* Then upsells additional products/services.
* Great for turning prospects into paying customers quickly. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Example:** $1 trial ‚Üí upsell. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **10. Video Sales Letter (VSL) Funnel**

* Uses **long-form video content** to sell your product.
* Acts like a persuasive sales pitch through video instead of text. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Best For:** Higher-ticket offers or complex products needing explanation. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **11. Cancellation Funnel**

* Designed to **retain customers who are cancelling** a recurring service.
* Ask why they are leaving and offer incentives to stay (discounts, upgrades).
* If they decline, collect feedback to improve future offerings. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Goal:** Learn from churn and reduce future cancellations. ([optimizepress.com][1])

---

## üìç **12. Live Demo Funnel**

* Provides **interactive product demonstrations** (often live or scheduled).
* Ideal for complex products (e.g., SaaS, software).
* Leads book demos, get personalised walkthroughs, and can convert with fewer objections. ([optimizepress.com][1])

**Typical Flow:**

1. Opt-in for demo
2. Schedule via calendar tool
3. One-on-one live demo
4. Follow-up and close. ([optimizepress.com][1])
