## The CLEAR Decision Model

### **C â€” Clarify the decision**

Define exactly what decision you're making.

Ask:

* What problem am I solving?
* What does "success" look like?
* By when must I decide?

ðŸ‘‰ *If you can't state the decision in one sentence, it's not clear yet.*

---

### **L â€” List options**

Generate **at least 3 options**, including:

* Do nothing
* The obvious choice
* One creative or extreme option

This prevents false binaries ("only A or B").

---

### **E â€” Evaluate with criteria**

Define **decision criteria** *before* choosing.

Typical criteria:

* Impact / value
* Risk
* Cost (money, time, energy)
* Reversibility
* Alignment with long-term goals

#### Simple scoring method

1. Assign weights (total = 100%)
2. Score each option (1â€“5 or 1â€“10)
3. Multiply score Ã— weight
4. Sum totals

This removes emotion from the comparison.

---

### **A â€” Assess risks & uncertainty**

For each option:

* What could go wrong?
* How likely is it?
* How bad would it be?

Use this rule:

> **Avoid decisions with irreversible downside unless upside is extraordinary.**

---

### **R â€” Review & decide**

Before finalizing, ask 5 quality-check questions:

1. Would I make the same decision tomorrow?
2. Does this still make sense if I'm wrong?
3. Am I avoiding something uncomfortable?
4. What would I advise a friend?
5. Is this decision reversible?

If answers are solid â†’ decide.

---

## Fast Version (30-Second Rule)

When time is limited:

> **Maximize long-term value, minimize irreversible regret.**

Ask:

* Is this reversible?
* Does it move me closer to my goals?
* Is fear driving this choice?

---

## High-Quality Decision Principles

### 1. Separate **decision quality** from **outcome**

Good decisions can have bad outcomes due to luck.
Bad decisions can succeed accidentally.

Judge decisions by **process**, not results.

---

### 2. Use the **80/20 rule**

* 80% of decisions are reversible â†’ decide fast
* 20% are irreversible â†’ decide slow

---

### 3. Avoid common decision traps

* **Confirmation bias**: seeking only supporting evidence
* **Status quo bias**: preferring no change
* **Sunk cost fallacy**: sticking because you already invested

---

### 4. Write it down

Writing forces clarity and reduces emotional distortion.
