## 4. Blunt Interpretation (No Sugarcoating)

### **Software engineering absolutely dominates hardware today**

Not because hardware is “bad”, but because:

### Hardware has **structural disadvantages**

* Fewer jobs globally
* Heavily tied to fabs, labs, visas, and geopolitics
* Long feedback cycles
* High specialization → low flexibility
* One bad choice early can lock you in

### Software has **structural advantages**

* Orders of magnitude more jobs
* Fast skill compounding
* Remote/global mobility
* Multiple exits: startup, big tech, research, product, freelancing
* **Most reversible career in engineering**

---

## 5. The Critical Insight (Most people miss this)

> **You can move from EC → Software easily**
> **You cannot move from Software → Core Hardware easily**

That alone should decide the direction.

---

## 6. Final Rule (Use this, not emotions)

### Choose **CS / Software** if you want:

* High probability of success
* Global freedom
* Faster financial independence
* Reversible career decisions
* Optionality

### Choose **EC / Hardware** *only if*:

* You are obsessed with circuits, chips, RF, VLSI
* You enjoy slow, deep, lab-based work
* You accept fewer jobs and higher barriers
* You’re okay with being geographically constrained

This is **identity-driven**, not opportunity-driven.
